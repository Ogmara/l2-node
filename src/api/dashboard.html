▼ What is anchoring?

Every hour your node computes a Merkle root of its current L2 state (users, channels, messages, anchors) and submits it to the Klever blockchain. When at least three independent nodes submit the same root for the same block height, that root becomes the network's canonical truth — provable on-chain, auditable by anyone.

  • Why it matters: tamper-evidence. Once canonical, the root cannot be revised without contradicting on-chain history.
  • What it costs: standard Klever transaction fees, paid by your node's anchorer wallet.
  • Who runs it: any registered node — including yours, after one-time on-chain registration.
